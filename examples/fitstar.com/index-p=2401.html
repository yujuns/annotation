Why Bodyweight Workouts Are Here to Stay

By Judi Brown — FitStar Contributor

As a personal trainer immersed in the fitness industry for close to 13 years, I’ve seen many exercise trends come and go. As a lifelong student of fitness, I’ve tried quite a few of them on for size. One of the most valuable lessons I’ve learned over my many years in the fitness world is that fitness fads come and go, but one constant remains: getting fit doesn’t have to be complicated. Phew, none of us have time for complicated.

Enter a not so new fitness trend: bodyweight workouts. What’s all the hub-bub about? Using our own bodyweight for exercise has been around for decades  so why are bodyweight workouts making a resurgence now? Bodyweight workouts ranked as one of the top fitness trends of 2014 for good reason. Simply put, bodyweight exercises can be a great choice for gaining strength, building muscle, boosting cardiovascular fitness and losing weight. They are convenient, efficient and cost effective. Bodyweight exercises are some of the most common and beneficial exercises that you can do. These exercises do not use free weights or any other type of machine or equipment. Instead, the person exercising uses his or her own bodyweight as the sole form of resistance for the workout. By including bodyweight exercises in your regular fitness routine – such as push-ups, crunches, and lunges – you can strengthen your body and lose weight.

One of the biggest misconceptions about exercising is that you need fancy shmancy equipment or an expensive gym membership. No equipment? No worries! The truth is all you need is a little space and a little motivation. Whether you are whittling your waist with Hard Core, or blasting calories with Boot Camp you will find out quickly that FitStar’s bodyweight workouts work.

So,why do WE really, really love bodyweight workouts so much?

Here are 5 reasons why bodyweight exercises are where it’s at:

1.  Convenience

One of the biggest advantages to bodyweight exercises is convenience.  Let’s face it, we are a society of convenience. We want fast and simple and convenient. I sure do. I’m a busy gal, a single mom of two little boys with no time to waste. I want to get my fitness in the most quick and efficient way.  Knowing that the most common reasons people give for not exercising is “there is no time” or “it’s inconvenient to go to the gym,” bodyweight workouts eliminate many of these excuses by allowing anyone to squeeze in workouts wherever they are. With bodyweight exercises there is no excuse to be a couch potato. In fact, you can march your bum right over to that comfy couch and use it as a bodyweight accessory. Try using it for dips or even a modified push-up.  Now the couch is transformed into a modified piece of exercise equipment! Bodyweight workouts can be used as a stress relieving break for those working from home or can provide a great hotel room workout for those on the road. “No time” really becomes no excuse. With FitStar, you can easily do a bodyweight workout anywhere, anytime.

2. Combined strength training and cardio

Not enough time in your hectic schedule and need to hit cardio and strength in one efficient workout? No problem. Performing quick bodyweight cardio moves (such as one minute of high knees or a set of mountain climbers) in between strength exercises (pushups, wall sit, plank) will keep the heart pumping while still encouraging muscle and strength development. Bodyweight-based exercises such as plyometrics yield awesome fitness gains in very short workout durations. Since there’s no equipment involved, bodyweight workouts make it easy to transition quickly from one exercise to the next. With shorter times to rest and recover, it’s easy to quickly boost the heart rate and burn serious calories while increasing muscle tone. FitStar’s Freestyle workout Alpine Blitz is great example as it features squat jumps, superman plank, and slalom jumps. You get your fat burning cardio, muscle sculpting and toning all in one!

3. Something for everyone

Bodyweight exercises can be modified to any fitness level and any age. Whether you’re a beginner or an elite athlete, you will reap the benefits. Adding repetitions, or performing the exercises faster or super-slow are a few ways to make even the simplest exercise more challenging. Increasing the number of repetitions adds to the total strength and endurance. Since bodyweight exercises use no weights, increasing resistance is accomplished in other ways. For example, a kneeling push-up can be ramped up by swapping it for a regular push-up or a push-up with a clap. Depending on what your fitness goals are, you can easily adjust your bodyweight workouts accordingly. And progress is easy to measure since bodyweight exercises offer tons of ways to do a little more in each workout.

4. Increased flexibility

Bodyweight training for strength and flexibility can go hand-in-hand. Completing bodyweight exercises through a full range of motion is a great way to ensure joints are moving freely. It can lead to improved posture and may even reduce the chance of exercise-related injury.  I love FitStar’s Freestyle workout Good Morning because it’s a wonderful way to work both strength and flexibility with moves like elbow plank and downward dog, and Training Camp is my personal favorite. Yoga, one of the more popular bodyweight-based workouts, is another great way to to improve flexibility while also significantly improving strength.

5.  Fun fitness on the cheap

Forget the same old routine, variety keeps things exciting. It can be easy to get stuck in a workout rut of bench presses and bicep curls. That’s why bodyweight training can be so invigorating; countless variations can spice up any workout routine. Working with a variety of exercises not only diminishes potential workout tedium, it can also help break through exercise plateaus to spark further fitness progress. The gym can get costly, so take FitStar with you anywhere for a fraction of the price – and with variety galore.
