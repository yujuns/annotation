3 Amazing Stats that Show How Fitness Impacts Your Heart

February is a great time to start thinking about your heart, and not just because Valentine’s Day is around the corner. February also happens to be American Heart Month, a month to evaluate how you’re treating your most vital organ.

With a bevy of new research coming out, it’s more apparent than ever before that fitness has an incredible impact on your risk of heart attack. We’re now finding that fitness may even be better than medication for people recovering from heart disease. Dr. Michael Blaha says that he hopes ”more physicians will consider prescribing physical activity as a front-line therapy”.

Now here is the really good news. The research shows that you don’t have to be an Iron Man triathlete to keep your heart safe; you just have to make sure you stay moderately active. Here are 3 facts that show the impact that a modest amount of activity can have on your heart’s well-being.

1.  Fit people have a 75% lower risk of dying from heart-related causes. This includes people with pre-existing heart conditions! Johns Hopkins recent study of 9800 adults found that an increase of one MET in exercise capacity was worth a 13% reduction in risk of death. This is kind of like the difference between being able to walk two miles per hour (2.3 METs) and three miles per hour (3.3 METs). Doable!

2.  Men who do regular cardio in their adolescence reduce their risk of heart attack later in life by 35%.  A study published in the European Heart Journal details how 740,000 men were followed for an average of 34 years to find, ultimately, that teenagers should definitely choose outdoor activities over video games if they yearn for a more heart attack-free life.

3.  People who maintain an active lifestyle have a 45% lower risk of developing heart disease than do sedentary people. According to The New York Times, the American Heart Association found that people benefitted from walking or jogging as few as 12 miles a week. Just think what you can do with all that Air Jump Rope during your Daily Dose!

February is a great month to keep after your New Year’s resolutions by reminding yourself that you’re not only losing weight and feeling energectic, but that you’re also keeping your heart as fit as it possibly can be. Take this information to heart!
