How to Blend Up the Best Green Smoothie

By Lori Rice — FitStar Contributor

Green smoothies have redefined healthy drinks by challenging the guidelines of what should be eaten and what should be sipped. Many people can’t quite embrace the idea of enjoying their leafy greens sweet and blended versus savory and sautéed. In case you’ve tried adding greens to smoothies and have been turned off, don’t give up. A balance needs to be achieved between leafy greens and other ingredients for an appealing flavor to help your green smoothie live up to the hype.

Benefits of Green Smoothies

It’s difficult to fault an effort to eat (or drink) more vegetables, especially when the result is delicious. There are plenty of reasons to grab your blender and start experimenting.

Vegetables for breakfast. These smoothies are most popular as breakfast and mid-morning snacks. They provide ways to increase vegetable intake at meals that aren’t notorious for including a wide variety of greens.

Boost of nutrients. If you are in a salad rut, tossing some greens into a smoothie provides a new way to get the nutritional benefits of raw vegetables.

The greens often go unnoticed. When combined with fruits, yogurts and milks, a handful of greens adds only a bright, fresh flavor. If you aren’t in love with the idea of eating more leafy vegetables, a green smoothie may be the most delicious option for getting more of their nutrients.

A smoothie feels like a treat. The creamy texture and sweet flavors of homemade smoothies are reminiscent of milk shakes. A green smoothie can be a nutritious way to fulfill a craving.

Best Greens for Your Smoothie

If you are new to these drinks, go easy on the vegetables at first. As you grow fond of the flavor (don’t worry, you will), gradually increase the amount you use and the variety to boost the nutritional benefits.

Spinach: Spinach is by far my favorite green for smoothies. It has a mild flavor that doesn’t overpower the other ingredients. It’s tender leaves puree completely so there are no tough pieces throughout the drink. One cup of loosely packed leaves is the perfect amount for most recipes.

Kale: Kale has a stronger flavor than spinach so I only use a couple leaves with the stems removed. Depending on the quality of your blender, it may chop very fine as opposed to pureeing completely. Kale goes best with berry smoothies or those with a chocolate flavor. Experiment with the different kinds of kale until you find one you like. My favorite is Lacinato or Dinosaur kale.

Arugula: This spicy green can add a pleasant balance to a super sweet smoothie. If you don’t enjoy arugula in salads, it may not be the best choice because you will taste it, but if you are a fan, try adding a small handful.

Herbs: Fresh herbs add a surprisingly delicious flavor. Start with just a few sprigs and try out some familiar combinations. For example, try fresh mint in a chocolate smoothie or basil in a smoothie with lemon or strawberry. Then consider trying some new options like parsley, lemon verbena, cilantro or a flavored mint like pineapple or chocolate. (Yes, these are plants you can add to your herb garden!)

Don’t Forget the Protein and Fiber

A handful of greens in a smoothie that is mostly fruit and juice isn’t going to balance out the effects of the sugar. Without added protein, you are headed for a rapid spike in blood glucose followed by crash that results in sluggishness and hunger. Commercial protein powders are an option for many people. I prefer to include as many natural ingredients in my smoothies as possible so I seek out other ways to boost protein. These are a few of the things I use on a regular basis.

• Nut butters
• Peanut flour
• Almond meal
• Milk – cow, goat or almond
• Rolled oats
• Cottage cheese
• Honey flavored yogurt

Sweet Tooth

Once you have your favorite ingredients combined, if you still find that your greens make the smoothie taste too bitter, a touch of sweetness can do the trick. Usually all you need is a teaspoon to a tablespoon of one of the sweeteners below to finish off the perfect green smoothie.

• Local honey
• Coconut milk or cream
• Coconut sugar
• Pure maple syrup
• Vanilla or honey flavored Greek yogurt
• Fresh squeezed orange juice
• Mashed banana
